Orders of Dress

Complete specifications for all British Army ceremonial and service uniforms.

Dress Order Specifications

The British Army maintains strict standards for all orders of dress. Each dress order has specific requirements for uniform components, accessories, and occasions of wear.

No.1 Dress (Ceremonial)

Who Can Wear It

  • Officers of the rank OF-2 and above.
  • Used for ceremonial occasions and parades.

Asset Regulations

  • Long Gloves: TRUE
  • Tattoos: FALSE
  • Watches: FALSE
  • Combat Gloves: FALSE
  • Medals: TRUE (Full laid-out medals)
  • Armbands: TRUE
  • Pins: TRUE
  • Neck Covers: Available for OF-7+

When It Is Worn

  • Parades, ceremonies, and formal occasions.
  • Church services and state functions.
  • Guard duties and public events.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Red tunic with brass buttons and regimental facings.
  • Trousers: Dark blue with red stripe.
  • Headwear: Peaked cap or bearskin.
  • Footwear: Black boots or shoes.

No.2 Dress (Service Dress)

Who Can Wear It

  • Officers of the rank OF-2 and above.
  • Used for semi-formal occasions.

Asset Regulations

  • Long Gloves: TRUE
  • Tattoos: FALSE
  • Watches: FALSE
  • Combat Gloves: FALSE
  • Medals: TRUE (Ribbons)
  • Armbands: TRUE
  • Pins: TRUE
  • Formal Cord: Left shoulder (Red/Blue/Orange)

When It Is Worn

  • Less formal parades and ceremonies.
  • Official visits and receptions.
  • Mess functions and social events.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Khaki service dress jacket.
  • Trousers: Khaki service dress trousers.
  • Headwear: Service dress cap.
  • Footwear: Black boots or shoes.

No.3 Dress (HQ Dress)

Who Can Wear It

  • Headquarters personnel (AEST Gen.+).
  • Allows full customization of pins and patches.

Asset Regulations

  • Long Gloves: TRUE
  • Tattoos: FALSE
  • Watches: FALSE
  • Combat Gloves: FALSE
  • Medals: TRUE (Full laid-out medals)
  • Armbands: TRUE
  • Pins: TRUE

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Open-necked bush jacket or HQ variant.
  • Trousers: Khaki drill trousers.
  • Headwear: Optional field service cap.

No.4 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • Senior Defence Staff member & 63xy15 are the only members within the military allowed to wear this uniform.
  • Warm Weather Service Dress

When It Is Worn

  • Less Ceremonial Occasions.
  • Tropical Weather.
  • General duties in operational areas.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Light Tan Coat.
  • Trousers: Light Tan Formal Dress Trousers.
  • Headwear: Formal No.1 Cap.
  • Footwear: Black Shoes.
  • Accessories: Golden aiguillette.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental shoulder flashes and arm badges.
  • Regimental lanyards and collar pins.

No.8 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All ranks during day to day basis.
  • Worn by British Army Personnel within the academy.

When It Is Worn

  • Standard day to day basis.
  • Short sleeved variants will be available during summer.
  • Long sleeved variants will be available during summer.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: No Coat.
  • Trousers: Forest Green Camo Pattern.
  • Shirt: British Army Rank on a Forest Green Camo Pattern.
  • Belt: Combat Belt.
  • Footwear: Brown Working Boots.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental Combat Belts.
  • Regimental Pins.
  • Regiment Abbreviation on Rank Slide.

No.13 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • all ranks during day to day basis during winter time.

When It Is Worn

  • all ranks during day to day basis during winter time.
  • Temperate Barrack Dress.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Short-sleeved bush jacket.
  • Trousers: Brown trousers.
  • Headwear: Beret For enlisted, Cap for Officers.
  • Footwear: Black Shoes.
  • Accessories: None.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental collar badges and shoulder flashes.

No.14 Dress (Formal Dress)

Who Can Wear It

  • Worn by all ranks OF-6 and above.
  • A more customizable formal alternative to No. 1s and No. 2s.

Asset Regulations

  • Tattoos: TRUE
  • Watches: TRUE
  • Medals: TRUE (Ribbons)
  • Pins: TRUE
  • Armbands: FALSE
  • Combat/Long Gloves: FALSE
  • Customizable Belts: TRUE (e.g., ETS Blue, RMP Red)

Uniform Details

  • Shirt: White formal shirt.
  • Trousers: Dark blue formal trousers.
  • Accessories: Regimental belts and chest pins.

Greatcoat & Frock Coat

Who Can Wear It

  • Rank of Major General (OF-7) and above.
  • Requires at least two British Army honors (George Medal to Victoria Cross).
  • Must be permitted by the Quartermaster General.

Asset Regulations

  • Gloves: Hand-only, Black color only.
  • Medals: Optional display of honors.
  • Watches/Tattoos: FALSE.

When It Is Worn

  • Ceremonial duties and medal ceremonies.
  • Winter ceremonial events.

AAC Flightsuit

Who Can Wear It

  • Army Air Corps personnel.
  • Aircrew and aviation support staff.

When It Is Worn

  • Flight operations and aviation duties.
  • Aviation training and maintenance.

Uniform Details

  • Suit: Green flightsuit with zip front.
  • Headwear: Flying helmet with communications.
  • Footwear: Flying boots.
  • Accessories: Life preserver, harness, and survival equipment.

Regimental Distinctions

  • AAC wings and qualification badges.
  • Rank insignia on flightsuit.
