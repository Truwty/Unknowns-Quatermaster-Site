Non-Ceremonial Uniforms

Official British Army specifications for non-ceremonial attire and daily wear.

Non-Ceremonial Standards

Non-ceremonial uniforms are worn for daily duties, training, and informal occasions. These uniforms maintain professional standards while providing comfort and functionality for operational requirements.

BA Standards Uniform

Who Can Wear It

  • All personnel ranked OR-2 and above.

When It Is Worn

  • General daily wear when not attending formal events or organized training.

Customization Options

  • Tattoos: Authorized.
  • Nametags: Authorized.
  • Watches: Authorized.
  • Sleeves: Can be rolled.
  • Belts: Entirely customizable based on regiment.
  • Badges: Customizable (e.g., IFD badges).
  • Armbands: Authorized.

Physical Training (PT) Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • Personnel attending regimental tryouts, parade deck events, or organized PT sessions.

Uniform Details

  • Sleeves: Always rolled.
  • Watches: Authorized.
  • Gloves: Authorized.
  • Tattoos: Authorized.

1 GFD Green Dress

Exclusive ceremonial dress for low-ranks and officers within the 1st Ground Forces Division. Worn by OF-7+ at Sandhurst.

Asset Regulations

  • Sashes: Authorized.

Barrack Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All ranks when off-duty or in barracks.

When It Is Worn

  • Within barracks and accommodation areas.
  • During off-duty hours and leisure time.
  • Not permitted for public wear or official duties.

Uniform Details

  • Top: Plain civilian-style clothing or approved leisure wear.
  • Bottom: Civilian trousers or approved leisure wear.
  • Footwear: Civilian shoes or approved leisure footwear.
  • Identification: Must carry military ID at all times.

Standards

  • Clothing must be clean, neat, and presentable.
  • No offensive or inappropriate items permitted.
  • Regimental affiliation may be displayed subtly.

Sports Kit

Who Can Wear It

  • All ranks during organized sports and recreational activities.

When It Is Worn

  • During regimental sports matches and competitions.
  • During organized recreational sports activities.

Uniform Details

  • Top: Regimental sports shirt or jersey.
  • Bottom: Sports shorts or tracksuit bottoms.
  • Footwear: Appropriate sports footwear.
  • Accessories: Sports-specific equipment as required.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental colors and identification on sports kit.
  • Team designations and player numbers.
  • Appropriate rank identification where required.
